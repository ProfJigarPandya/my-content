<p><b>Tessy Thomas</b> (born in 1963) is an <a href="/wiki/Engineer" title="Engineer">engineer</a> and the Project Director for <a href="/wiki/Agni-IV" title="Agni-IV">Agni-IV</a> missile in <a href="/wiki/Defence_Research_and_Development_Organisation" title="Defence Research and Development Organisation">Defence Research and Development Organisation</a>. She is the first woman engineer to head a missile project in <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>. She is known as the 'Missile Woman' of India.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_Education">Early life and Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tessy_Thomas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tessy was born in April 1963 in <a href="/wiki/Alappuzha" title="Alappuzha">Alappuzha</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kerala" title="Kerala">Kerala</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> to a small-businessman father and a homemaker mother. She graduated in <a href="/wiki/Engineering" title="Engineering">engineering</a> from <a href="/wiki/Government_Engineering_College,_Thrissur" title="Government Engineering College, Thrissur">Government Engineering College, Thrissur</a>. She grew up near a rocket launching station and says her fascination with rockets and missiles began then. She also has an M.Tech in Guided Missile from the Institute of Armament Technology, Pune (now known as the <a href="/wiki/Defence_Institute_of_Advanced_Technology" title="Defence Institute of Advanced Technology">Defence Institute of Advanced Technology</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-IWSA_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IWSA-2">[2]</a></sup> Her parents from Kerala named her after Mother Teresa.<sup id="cite_ref-IndiaToday_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IndiaToday-3">[3]</a></sup> <sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tessy_Thomas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tessy was associate project director of the 3,000&#160;km range <a href="/wiki/Agni-III" title="Agni-III">Agni-III</a> missile project.<sup id="cite_ref-kerala_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kerala-5">[5]</a></sup> She was the project director for mission <a href="/wiki/Agni-IV" title="Agni-IV">Agni IV</a> which was successfully tested in 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Tessy was appointed as the Project Director for 5,000&#160;km range <a href="/wiki/Agni-V" title="Agni-V">Agni-V</a> in 2009 and is based The missile was successfully tested on 19 April 2012. In January 2012, Prime Minister Manmohan Singh told the Indian Science Congress that Mrs Thomas is an example of a "woman making her mark in a traditionally male bastion and decisively breaking the glass ceiling". The media loves to call her Agniputri, or one born of fire, after the missiles she has helped develop. "We are all proud of our country. Agni-V is one of our greatest achievements," she says.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tessy_Thomas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>She is married to Saroj Kumar, now a commander in the Indian Navy and they have a son, Tejas.<sup id="cite_ref-IWSA_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IWSA-2">[2]</a></sup> engineer in Ford Technologies.In a glowing tribute in 2008, The Indian Woman Scientists Association did not forget to mention that "like most women she also does a tight-rope walk between home and career, between being a mother and a scientist who is dedicated to her job. We feel Tessy Thomas serves as a role model and an inspiration for women scientists to achieve their dreams and have their feet planted in both worlds successfully," the group said.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards">Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tessy_Thomas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tessy Thomas was conferred the <a href="/wiki/Lal_Bahadur_Shastri_National_Award" title="Lal Bahadur Shastri National Award">Lal Bahadur Shastri National Award</a> for her outstanding contribution for making <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> self-reliant in the field of <a href="/wiki/Integrated_Guided_Missile_Development_Program" class="mw-redirect" title="Integrated Guided Missile Development Program">missile technology</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Tessy Thomas was awarded <a href="/wiki/D.Sc." class="mw-redirect" title="D.Sc.">D.Sc.</a> (Honoris causa) by <a href="/wiki/ITM_University,_Gwalior" class="mw-redirect" title="ITM University, Gwalior">ITM University, Gwalior</a> in 2016.</p>
