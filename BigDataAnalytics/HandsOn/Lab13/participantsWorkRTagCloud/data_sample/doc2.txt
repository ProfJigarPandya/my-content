<p><b>Sonu Nigam</b> (born 30 July 1973)<sup id="cite_ref-dob_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dob-2">[2]</a></sup> is an <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">Indian</a> <a href="/wiki/Playback_singer" title="Playback singer">playback singer</a> whose songs have been featured mainly in <a href="/wiki/Hindi" title="Hindi">Hindi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kannada" title="Kannada">Kannada</a>. He has also sung in <a href="/wiki/Gujarati_language" title="Gujarati language">Gujarati</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tamil_language" title="Tamil language">Tamil</a>, <a href="/wiki/Telugu_language" title="Telugu language">Telugu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marathi_language" title="Marathi language">Marathi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tulu_language" title="Tulu language">Tulu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Assamese_language" title="Assamese language">Assamese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Odia_language" title="Odia language">Odia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nepali_language" title="Nepali language">Nepali</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maithili_language" title="Maithili language">Maithili</a> and various Indian languages. He has also released <a href="/wiki/Indian_pop" title="Indian pop">Indian pop</a> albums and acted in a number of films.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> He has been one of the highest paid Indian singers. His singing style is considered similar to the legendary <a href="/wiki/Mohd._Rafi" class="mw-redirect" title="Mohd. Rafi">Mohd. Rafi</a> whom he also considers his motivation. He has come into limelight through the <a href="/wiki/Kannada" title="Kannada">Kannada</a> film <i><a href="/wiki/Mungaru_Male" title="Mungaru Male">Mungaru Male</a></i> (2006), which set several records in <a href="/wiki/Indian_cinema" class="mw-redirect" title="Indian cinema">Indian cinema</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_and_personal_life">Early and personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sonu_Nigam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early and personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sonu Agam Kumar Nigam was born on 30 July 1973 to Agam Kumar Nigam and Shobha Agam Kumar Nigam in the city of <a href="/wiki/Faridabad" title="Faridabad">Faridabad</a>, <a href="/wiki/Haryana" title="Haryana">Haryana</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Nigam began his singing career at the age of four, when he joined his father Agam Kumar Nigam on stage to sing <a href="/wiki/Mohammed_Rafi" title="Mohammed Rafi">Mohammed Rafi</a>'s song "Kya hua tera wada".<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> From then on, Nigam accompanied his father on his singing appearances at weddings and parties. He moved to <a href="/wiki/Mumbai" title="Mumbai">Mumbai</a> with his father to begin his <a href="/wiki/Bollywood" title="Bollywood">Bollywood</a> singing career at the age of 18.<sup id="cite_ref-earlyyears_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-earlyyears-9">[9]</a></sup> He was trained by Hindustani classical singer <a href="/wiki/Ghulam_Mustafa_Khan_(singer)" title="Ghulam Mustafa Khan (singer)">Ustad Ghulam Mustafa Khan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Nigam married Madhurima Agam Kumar Nigam on 15 February 2002.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>

<p>He has recorded Romantic, Rock, Sad, and Patriotic songs in: <a href="/wiki/Hindi" title="Hindi">Hindi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kannada" title="Kannada">Kannada</a>, <a href="/wiki/Assamese_language" title="Assamese language">Assamese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bengali_language" title="Bengali language">Bengali</a>, <a href="/wiki/Odia_language" title="Odia language">Odia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Punjabi_language" title="Punjabi language">Punjabi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tamil_language" title="Tamil language">Tamil</a>, <a href="/wiki/Telugu_language" title="Telugu language">Telugu</a>, <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maithili_language" title="Maithili language">Maithili</a> , <a href="/wiki/Bhojpuri_language" title="Bhojpuri language">Bhojpuri</a>, <a href="/wiki/Urdu_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Urdu language">Urdu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nepali_language" title="Nepali language">Nepali</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chhattisgarhi_language" title="Chhattisgarhi language">Chhattisgarhi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Malayalam_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Malayalam language">Malayalam</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tulu_language" title="Tulu language">Tulu</a> and <a href="/wiki/Marathi_language" title="Marathi language">Marathi</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-earlyyears_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-earlyyears-9">[9]</a></sup> Nigam has released pop albums in <a href="/wiki/Hindi" title="Hindi">Hindi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kannada" title="Kannada">Kannada</a>, <a href="/wiki/Odia_language" title="Odia language">Odia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chhattisgarhi_language" title="Chhattisgarhi language">Chhattisgarhi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Punjabi_language" title="Punjabi language">Punjabi</a>, as well as <a href="/wiki/Hindu" title="Hindu">Hindu</a> and Islamic devotional albums. He has released several <a href="/w/index.php?title=Buddhist_albums&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Buddhist albums (page does not exist)">Buddhist albums</a>.</p>
<p>Nigam has performed in countries in North <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">America</a>, <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, the Middle East, <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>. In May–June 2007, he participated in The Incredibles tour with <a href="/wiki/Asha_Bhosle" title="Asha Bhosle">Asha Bhosle</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kunal_Ganjawala" title="Kunal Ganjawala">Kunal Ganjawala</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Kailash_Kher" title="Kailash Kher">Kailash Kher</a>. In September–October of the same year, he gave solo concerts titled Simply Sonu in <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> and <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, the first Indian singer to do so.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> In April 2008, he toured <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> promoting his Punjabi single "Punjabi Please".<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>Nigam's first film song as a playback singer was for <i><a href="/wiki/Janam" title="Janam">Janam</a></i> (1990), which was never officially released. He then began making radio commercials and also acted in some of them.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>His first released song was <b>"O Aasman Wale"</b> from the movie <i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aaja_Meri_Jaan&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Aaja Meri Jaan (page does not exist)">Aaja Meri Jaan</a></i> (1992). Sonu had also sung title Track Of Aaja Meri Jaan. But it Was Dubbed By SP Balasubramaniam. He released his first album "<a href="/w/index.php?title=Rafi_Ki_Yaadein&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Rafi Ki Yaadein (page does not exist)">Rafi Ki Yaadein</a>" in 1992. After this he sung in some movies included as <i><a href="/wiki/Muqabla" class="mw-redirect" title="Muqabla">Muqabla</a></i> (1993), <i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Meharban&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Meharban (page does not exist)">Meharban</a></i> (1993), <i><a href="/wiki/Shabnam" title="Shabnam">Shabnam</a></i> (1993), <i><a href="/wiki/Aag_(1994_film)" title="Aag (1994 film)">Aag</a></i> (1994), <i><a href="/wiki/Khuddar" title="Khuddar">Khuddar</a></i> (1994), <i><a href="/wiki/Hulchul_(1995_film)" title="Hulchul (1995 film)">Hulchul</a></i> (1994), <i><a href="/wiki/Stuntman" class="mw-redirect" title="Stuntman">Stuntman</a></i> (1994), <i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ramjaane&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ramjaane (page does not exist)">Ramjaane</a></i> (1995), <a href="/wiki/Gaddar" title="Gaddar">Gaddar</a> (1995) etc. Sonu Nigam was supposed to playback the song Ek Ladki Ko Dekha Toh from 1942 A Love Story And Sonu Was The First Choice Of RD Burman For This Song But Unfortunately This Song Was Recorded By Kumar Sanu. He began hosting the TV show, <i><a href="/wiki/Sa_Re_Ga_Ma" class="mw-redirect" title="Sa Re Ga Ma">Sa Re Ga Ma</a></i> in 1995 and sang "Accha Sila Diya" for the film <i>Bewafa Sanam</i> and got a big success through this song. In the same year.<sup id="cite_ref-earlyyears_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-earlyyears-9">[9]</a></sup> he sang the <a href="/wiki/Anu_Malik" title="Anu Malik">Anu Malik</a>-composed song "<a href="/wiki/Sandese_Aate_Hai" title="Sandese Aate Hai">Sandese Aate Hai</a>" in <i><a href="/wiki/Border_(1997_film)" title="Border (1997 film)">Border</a></i> and performed the <a href="/wiki/Nadeem-Shravan" class="mw-redirect" title="Nadeem-Shravan">Nadeem-Shravan</a>-composed song "Yeh Dil Deewana" in <i><a href="/wiki/Pardes_(film)" title="Pardes (film)">Pardes</a></i> in 1997. Nigam's album <i><a href="/wiki/Deewana_(album)" class="mw-redirect" title="Deewana (album)">Deewana</a></i>, with music directed by Sajid-Wajid, was released by T-Series in 1999.<sup id="cite_ref-earlyyears_9-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-earlyyears-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Nigam provided playback singing for Hindi movies including the title song for the film <i><a href="/wiki/Kal_Ho_Naa_Ho" title="Kal Ho Naa Ho">Kal Ho Naa Ho</a></i> in 2003 and "Abhi Mujhme Kahin" for <i>Agneepath</i> in 2012.</p>
<p>He composed the title track of the film <i><a href="/wiki/Singh_Saab_The_Great" class="mw-redirect" title="Singh Saab The Great">Singh Saab The Great</a></i> in 2013 and has also composed music for other films including: <i><a href="/wiki/Sooper_Se_Ooper" title="Sooper Se Ooper">Sooper Se Ooper</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Jal_(film)" title="Jal (film)">Jal</a></i>, in collaboration with percussionist <a href="/wiki/Bickram_Ghosh" title="Bickram Ghosh">Bickram Ghosh</a> in the same year.</p>
<p>Nigam has released albums of <a href="/wiki/Mohammed_Rafi" title="Mohammed Rafi">Mohammed Rafi</a>'s songs including <i>Rafi Ki Yaadein</i> a collection early in his career, which was re-released in Rafi's memory in September 2007 as a six-disc collection of 100 songs, titled <i>Kal Aaj Aur Kal</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> In 2008, soon after releasing <i>Classically Mild</i>, he released a single Punjabi track entitled "Punjabi Please"<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> and <i>Rafi Resurrected</i>, a two-disc collection of Rafi songs with music by the <a href="/wiki/City_of_Birmingham_Symphony_Orchestra" title="City of Birmingham Symphony Orchestra">Birmingham Symphony Orchestra</a>. He contributed to the lyrics of several songs and directed the music for his album <i><a href="/wiki/Chanda_Ki_Doli" title="Chanda Ki Doli">Chanda Ki Doli</a></i>.</p>
<p>After the death of <a href="/wiki/Michael_Jackson" title="Michael Jackson">Michael Jackson</a>, Nigam released a song as a tribute to Jackson which was included on the tribute album <i>The Beat of Our Hearts</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>In November 2007, at the inauguration of <a href="/wiki/Harvard_University" title="Harvard University">Harvard</a> University's 28th president, <a href="/wiki/Drew_Gilpin_Faust" title="Drew Gilpin Faust">Drew Gilpin Faust</a>, Nigam sang <a href="/wiki/Mohandas_Karamchand_Gandhi" class="mw-redirect" title="Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi">Mahatma Gandhi</a>'s favorite <a href="/wiki/Bhajan" title="Bhajan">bhajan</a>, "Vaishnav Jan To Tene Kahiye", with the Harvard College Sangeet.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>In July 2008, he participated in a three-city tour of the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, singing Rafi songs with the City of Birmingham Symphony Orchestra (CBSO) . This followed the release of these songs by the CBSO and the Indian music company <a href="/wiki/Sa_Re_Ga_Ma" class="mw-redirect" title="Sa Re Ga Ma">Sa Re Ga Ma</a> on <i>Rafi Resurrected</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>He participated in a <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">US</a> tour with <a href="/wiki/Sunidhi_Chauhan" title="Sunidhi Chauhan">Sunidhi Chauhan</a> in 2009 called The Explosion 2009 tour and in a <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">UK</a> tour titled All Izz Well in November 2010. In 2011, Nigam collaborated with Kakas Entertainment and <a href="/wiki/Laxmikant-Pyarelal" class="mw-redirect" title="Laxmikant-Pyarelal">Laxmikant-Pyarelal</a> for a Maestros Concert performing Mohammed Rafi songs.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2011 Nigam collaborated with <a href="/wiki/Britney_Spears" title="Britney Spears">Britney Spears</a> on a remix of her "<a href="/wiki/I_Wanna_Go" title="I Wanna Go">I Wanna Go</a>" track,<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> and with DJ <a href="/wiki/Avicii" title="Avicii">Avicii</a> on his song "Indian Levels" produced by Avicii in 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2014 Nigam released an online only track, "Trini Ladki".</p>
<p>He was ranked the Number 1 artist on the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">US</a> <a href="/wiki/Uncharted_(chart)" title="Uncharted (chart)">Billboard Uncharted</a> charts twice in September and October 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>In September 2015 Nigam recorded a song with the music director <a href="/wiki/Khayyam" class="mw-redirect" title="Khayyam">Khayyam</a> for the upcoming project titled <i>Gulam Bandhu</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Indian_Express_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Indian_Express-25">[25]</a></sup> Sonu Has Sang Around 1100 Songs In Hindi Language.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Work_in_Kannada">Work in Kannada</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sonu_Nigam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Work in Kannada">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:313px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Sonu_Nigam122.jpg" class="image"><img alt="Sonu Nigam singing in the concert" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/2/29/Sonu_Nigam122.jpg" width="311" height="267" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="311" data-file-height="267" /></a>

<p>Nigam's first song in <a href="/wiki/Kannada" title="Kannada">Kannada</a> was in 1996 for the film <i><a href="/wiki/Jeevanadhi" title="Jeevanadhi">Jeevanadhi</a></i>. The song "Yello Yaro Hego" was composed by <a href="/wiki/Saluri_Koteswara_Rao" title="Saluri Koteswara Rao">Saluri Koteswara Rao</a> with lyrics by <a href="/wiki/R._N._Jayagopal" title="R. N. Jayagopal">R. N. Jayagopal</a>. Nigam has since sung over 600 Kannada songs.<sup id="cite_ref-deccanherald.com_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-deccanherald.com-26">[26]</a></sup> He is known as the The Golden Singer Of Karnataka.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>His songs include "Cheluve Yeke Bande" from the film <i>Majnu</i> composed by <a href="/wiki/Gurukiran" title="Gurukiran">Gurukiran</a>; "Titanic Heroine nee nanna cheluve" from the film <i><a href="/wiki/Snehaloka" title="Snehaloka">Snehaloka</a></i>; the title track from the film <i><a href="/wiki/Monalisa_(film)" title="Monalisa (film)">Monalisa</a></i> composed by Valisha Sandeep;<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> "Hamsave Hamsave" from the film <i>Gatti mela</i> composed by <a href="/wiki/Hamsalekha" title="Hamsalekha">Hamsalekha</a>, "Kannale Kannale" from the film <i><a href="/wiki/Aham_Premasmi" title="Aham Premasmi">Aham Premasmi</a></i> composed by <a href="/wiki/V._Ravichandran" title="V. Ravichandran">V.Ravichandran</a><sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> and "Baaninda Baa Chandira from the movie <i>Kanti</i> composed by <a href="/wiki/Gurukiran" title="Gurukiran">Gurukiran</a>. The 2006 film <i><a href="/wiki/Mungaru_Male" title="Mungaru Male">Mungaru Male</a></i> created a sensation and spawned several records, mainly in Karnataka.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p>The songs "Mungaru Maleye" and "<a href="/wiki/Anisuthide" title="Anisuthide">Anisuthide</a>" were composed by <a href="/wiki/Mano_Murthy" title="Mano Murthy">Mano Murthy</a> and written by <a href="/wiki/Yogaraj_Bhat" title="Yogaraj Bhat">Yogaraj Bhat</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jayant_Kaikini" class="mw-redirect" title="Jayant Kaikini">Jayant Kaikini</a> respectively. Nigam has said that he finds Kannada songs more fulfilling than the Hindi numbers.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> In an interview with <i><a href="/wiki/Deccan_Herald" title="Deccan Herald">Deccan Herald</a></i> he said "Kannada songs make me feel positive".<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> He went on to say: "I highly respect the musicians of <a href="/wiki/Karnataka" title="Karnataka">Karnataka</a> and many of my favourite songs are in Kannada. I consider <a href="/wiki/Bangalore" title="Bangalore">Bangalore</a> as my second birthplace. Even if someone requests me to sing a Kannada song while I'm performing in the West Indies, I will sing it for them".<sup id="cite_ref-deccanherald.com_26-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-deccanherald.com-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>From the same <i>Mungaru Male</i> combination, composer Murthy, lyricist Kaikini and Nigam got together to record a Kannada album <i><a href="/wiki/Neene_Bari_Neene" title="Neene Bari Neene">Neene Bari Neene</a></i> produced by <a href="/wiki/Ashok_Kheny" title="Ashok Kheny">Ashok Kheny</a>. Videos for the songs "Neene Bari Neene" and "Baa Nodu Gelathi" were shot on the outskirts of <a href="/wiki/Bangalore" title="Bangalore">Bengaluru</a> featuring Nigam and model <a href="/wiki/Madhuri_Bhattacharya" title="Madhuri Bhattacharya">Madhuri Bhattacharya</a> in the lead.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> Nigam also composed a theme song for the <a href="/wiki/Karnataka_Bulldozers" title="Karnataka Bulldozers">Karnataka Bulldozers</a> team in the <a href="/wiki/Celebrity_Cricket_League" title="Celebrity Cricket League">Celebrity Cricket League</a> and sang it with <a href="/wiki/Kunal_Ganjawala" title="Kunal Ganjawala">Kunal Ganjawala</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sowmya_Raoh" title="Sowmya Raoh">Sowmya Raoh</a>, who also penned the lyrics.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Work_as_a_host_and_celebrity_judge">Work as a host and celebrity judge</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sonu_Nigam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Work as a host and celebrity judge">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nigam was the host of <i><a href="/wiki/Sa_Re_Ga_Ma" class="mw-redirect" title="Sa Re Ga Ma">Sa Re Ga Ma</a></i> music show from 1995 until 1999.<sup id="cite_ref-bbc_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-39">[39]</a></sup> He returned on <i><a href="/wiki/Sa_Re_Ga_Ma_Pa_L%27il_Champs_International" title="Sa Re Ga Ma Pa L'il Champs International">Sa Re Ga Ma Pa L'il Champs International</a></i> as a judge with <a href="/wiki/Suresh_Wadkar" title="Suresh Wadkar">Suresh Wadkar</a> in October 2007. Nigam was a celebrity judge on the <i><a href="/wiki/Sa_Re_Ga_Ma_Pa_Mega_Challenge" title="Sa Re Ga Ma Pa Mega Challenge">Sa Re Ga Ma Pa Mega Challenge</a></i> grand finale on 12 December 2009 .</p>
<p>Nigam hosted the TV show <i>Kisme Kitna Hai Dum</i> on <a href="/wiki/Star_Plus" title="Star Plus">Star Plus</a> in 2002.</p>
<p>He appeared as the judge on <i><a href="/wiki/Indian_Idol" title="Indian Idol">Indian Idol</a></i> in seasons 1 (October 2004&#160;– March 2005) and 2 (November 2005&#160;– April 2006), and was a celebrity judge in seasons 3 (17 August 2007) and 4 (16 January 2009 and 6 February 2009).</p>
<p>In 2006, Nigam hosted <i>Life Ki Dhun with Sonu Nigaam</i> on <a href="/wiki/Radio_City_(Indian_radio_station)" title="Radio City (Indian radio station)">Radiocity 91.1 FM</a>, interviewing musicians.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<p>He appeared as a celebrity judge on <i><a href="/wiki/Amul_STAR_Voice_of_India" class="mw-redirect" title="Amul STAR Voice of India">Amul STAR Voice of India</a></i> in August 2007 (Season 1) and in December 2008 (Season 2), and on the grand finale of music reality show <i><a href="/wiki/Jo_Jeeta_Wohi_Super_Star" title="Jo Jeeta Wohi Super Star">Jo Jeeta Wohi Super Star</a></i> on 12 July 2008.</p>
<p>Nigam was a judge/mentor on <i><a href="/wiki/Chhote_Ustaad" title="Chhote Ustaad">Chhote Ustaad</a> – Do Deshon Ki Ek Awaaz</i> (July&#160;– October, 2010) with <a href="/wiki/Rahat_Nusrat_Fateh_Ali_Khan" class="mw-redirect" title="Rahat Nusrat Fateh Ali Khan">Rahat Nusrat Fateh Ali Khan</a>, and was a judge/mentor on the first season of <i><a href="/wiki/X_Factor_(India)" class="mw-redirect" title="X Factor (India)">X Factor (India)</a></i> (29 May&#160;– 2 September 2011) along with <a href="/wiki/Sanjay_Leela_Bhansali" title="Sanjay Leela Bhansali">Sanjay Leela Bhansali</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shreya_Ghoshal" title="Shreya Ghoshal">Shreya Ghoshal</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-bbc_39-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 23 August 2015, he appeared on "<a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Anupam_Kher_Show_%E2%80%93_Kucch_Bhi_Ho_Sakta_Hai&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="The Anupam Kher Show – Kucch Bhi Ho Sakta Hai (page does not exist)">The Anupam Kher Show</a>" sharing his life experiences. Currently he is one of the three judges of Indian Idol 2016–17.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Film_and_voice-over_acting">Film and voice-over acting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sonu_Nigam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Film and voice-over acting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nigam's acting career<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> started as a child in films including <i><a href="/wiki/Betaab" title="Betaab">Betaab</a></i> (1983). As an adult, he acted in films such as <i><a href="/wiki/Jaani_Dushman:_Ek_Anokhi_Kahani" title="Jaani Dushman: Ek Anokhi Kahani">Jaani Dushman: Ek Anokhi Kahani</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Kash_Aap_Hamare_Hote" title="Kash Aap Hamare Hote">Kash Aap Hamare Hote</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Love_in_Nepal" title="Love in Nepal">Love in Nepal</a></i>. He has been signed to be in <i>Ankhon Ankhon Mein</i>, about a blind singer.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> Nigam provided the <a href="/wiki/Voice-over" title="Voice-over">voice</a> for the lead character, in the Hindi translated version of <i><a href="/wiki/Aladdin_(1992_Disney_film)" title="Aladdin (1992 Disney film)">Aladdin</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-disney_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-disney-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
